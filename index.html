Basic HTML Is the New Punk Folk Explosion
written by: zack mandeville / a reading response

The internet was a place that I always thought of as special and magical.
A place where people were free to explore . . .
free to express . . .
free to connect . . .
(where I was able to enter disney.com or nickelodeon.com and felt super cool)
Where websites took an eternity to load . . .

i was only thirteen years old. . .
And then came . . .
Facebook.
Then Instagram.
Then Twitter.
Then Tumblr.
Then Snapchat.
Then Instagram videos.
Then. Then. Then. Then. Then. Then. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

—
“I do it because I think it could be a useful alternative to
Facebook and the fatigue of the mainstream world."

Something he said that I thought was interesting was the idea of a website feeling homemade.
Where websites do not need to look like the platforms we see today.
And that websites can be anything, and should be anything.
Learning from the aesthetics of giant corporations to then learn how to make a website from scratch is a type of punk.

“There are people who are far younger than the web, who never knew a world without the internet. If a teen is drawn to early web aesthetics, it is not out of nostalgia for their youth, it is instead a yearning for a time from before they even existed, a time they can only imagine."

“So building a website from scratch, with hard-coded gif animations and a web-ring, is now part of the back-to-the-land tradition."
